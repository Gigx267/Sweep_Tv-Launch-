President Bola Ahmed Tinubu departs for UK State Visit with high-level delegation

Published March 18, 2026 · updated 1 hour ago | By Sweep TV foreign desk

On March 17, 2026, President Bola Ahmed Tinubu departed Abuja for a state visit to the United Kingdom, accompanied by the First Lady, Senator Oluremi Tinubu, and a high-level government delegation. The visit aims to strengthen bilateral relations, trade, and investment partnerships between Nigeria and the United Kingdom.

The presidential aircraft departed the Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport at approximately 10:30 am local time. The delegation includes top government officials, legislators, and ministers, reflecting the strategic importance of the visit.

Purpose of the visit

According to a statement from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the state visit is at the invitation of the British government. Talks will focus on deepening economic ties, security cooperation, technology exchange, and cultural partnerships. Several MOUs in trade, investment, and creative industries are expected to be signed during the visit.

President Tinubu is scheduled to meet with Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, senior members of the royal family, and business leaders from the UK-Nigeria investment council. The delegation also includes a strong economic team to attract foreign direct investment into Nigeria’s solid minerals, digital economy, and infrastructure sectors.

This is President Tinubu's second official visit to the UK since taking office, underscoring the importance of the bilateral relationship. The First Lady, Senator Oluremi Tinubu, will participate in separate engagements focused on education, health, and women's empowerment.
