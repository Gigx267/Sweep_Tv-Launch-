Lagos 2027: APC Big Names Emerge as Governorship Race Begins

Published March 24, 2026 · updated 2 hours ago | By Sweep TV political bureau — Lagos
Today's exclusive report: Barely 20 months before the 2027 gubernatorial election, high-stakes maneuvering has already gripped Lagos APC. At least five heavyweight figures — including a former governor, a serving deputy governor, a sitting minister, and top presidential aides — are being tipped as potential successors. While no official declarations have been made, sources reveal intense behind-the-scenes lobbying and alliance-building.

A new political season is quietly unfolding in Nigeria’s commercial nerve centre. Even though the 2027 Lagos State governorship election remains about 22 months away, the battle to succeed the incumbent governor has already begun. Within the All Progressives Congress (APC) — the party that has governed Lagos since 1999 — multiple influential names are surfacing as frontline aspirants. From former governor Akinwunmi Ambode to Deputy Governor Obafemi Hamzat, Senator Tokunbo Abiru, Minister of Education Tunji Alausa, and Hakeem Muri-Okunola (Principal Secretary to the President), the contest promises to be a defining moment for Lagos politics and the ruling party’s internal cohesion.

The heavyweights: five contenders shaping the conversation

Akinwunmi Ambode
Former Governor (2015–2019)

Ambode’s possible return has sparked intense debate. Political veterans see a strategic bid to reclaim the centre after his 2019 primary loss. Known for infrastructural feats, his name commands loyalty among grassroots networks.

Obafemi Hamzat
Deputy Governor, Lagos State

As the incumbent deputy, Hamzat is widely seen as a natural heir. He enjoys strong administrative experience and is backed by key party elders who value continuity and technocratic governance.

Tokunbo Abiru
Senator, Lagos East

A former banker and respected technocrat, Abiru commands influence across the Lagos East senatorial district and appeals to the professional class. His legislative record boosts his credentials.

Tunji Alausa
Minister of Education

Alausa’s federal cabinet role gives him national visibility. His influence within the Lagos APC structures has grown, with supporters arguing that his network across local governments is formidable.

Hakeem Muri-Okunola
Principal Secretary to the President

Former Head of Service in Lagos, Muri-Okunola is seen as a cerebral administrator. His proximity to the presidency and deep knowledge of Lagos bureaucracy make him a wildcard contender.

Inside the early race: alliances, factions, and the power of incumbency

Political sources confide that the emerging contest is layered with complexity. While the incumbent governor is constitutionally limited to two terms, his succession will define the party’s trajectory in Lagos — a state where the APC has enjoyed uninterrupted rule but now faces rising opposition energy. Each aspirant is silently building coalitions: Ambode is reaching out to 2019-era loyalists; Hamzat benefits from daily governance visibility; Abiru consolidates support in the legislative caucus; Alausa leverages federal appointments; and Muri-Okunola attracts progressive-minded technocrats.

A key insider who spoke with Sweep TV noted: “Lagos is too strategic for a chaotic primary. The party leadership is already holding informal meetings. 2027 will test the strength of zoning conventions, internal democracy, and the balance between continuity and fresh leadership.”

The road to 2027: early signals & strategic calculations

March 2026
APC chieftains begin quiet consultations. Names like Ambode, Hamzat, Abiru, Alausa, and Muri-Okunola circulate in political circles. Pro-aspirant groups emerge on social media, testing public sentiment.
Late 2026
Expected formal declarations and intensified ward-level mobilisation. Zoning debate — whether Lagos should retain power in Lagos West, Central or shift — will shape party harmony.
Early 2027
APC primaries scheduled. With multiple heavyweights, the contest may become a litmus test for the party’s conflict-resolution mechanisms. A consensus arrangement or direct primary looms.
2027 General Election
Governorship election set to hold alongside presidential polls. The eventual APC candidate will face a rejuvenated opposition, making grassroots popularity and economic messaging critical.
“Lagos is the flagship APC state; the governorship race must reflect the diversity, competence, and capacity to sustain development. We are monitoring developments, but we trust the party will produce a candidate who can win resoundingly in 2027.”
— Sen. Ganiyu Olanrewaju Solomon (Lagos APC chieftain, March 24, 2026)

Political analysts: “Early jostling reveals party fault lines”

Political commentator Dr. Fatima Bello notes: “The emergence of these five figures so early signals that the Lagos APC is bracing for its most competitive primary in a decade. Ambode’s potential return is particularly symbolic — it could either unify or deepen old wounds from 2019. Hamzat represents continuity; Abiru and Alausa appeal to the reformist wing; Muri-Okunola is the outsider-insider. The party’s ability to manage this diversity will determine whether Lagos remains a fortress.”

Moreover, analysts point out that any candidate will need to address key voter concerns: transportation infrastructure, housing affordability, education, and job creation. The Lagos electorate has grown more sophisticated, demanding tangible results over party loyalty alone.

Civil society: “Focus on issue-based campaigns”

The Lagos Civil Society Assembly issued a statement calling on aspirants to move beyond personality politics. “We urge all potential candidates to present clear manifestos. Lagos needs a leader committed to tackling climate resilience, transport innovation, and inclusive economic growth. The 2027 election must be about solutions, not just party machinery.”

Zoning equation: a decisive factor in Lagos APC

One of the most sensitive subplots is zoning. Since 1999, Lagos governorship has rotated between the three senatorial districts: Lagos West, Lagos Central, and Lagos East. The current governor hails from Lagos West. Stakeholders are divided on whether the next candidate should come from Lagos Central or Lagos East to maintain rotation equity. Senator Tokunbo Abiru (Lagos East) and Deputy Governor Hamzat (Lagos West) reflect the ongoing debate. Party elders may lean toward a consensus arrangement to avoid a divisive primary. This debate is likely to intensify in the coming months, with aspirants making quiet but strategic appeals to kingmakers across the state.

Lagos governance milestones (2023–2026)
  • Completion of the Red Line rail project phase 1, boosting mass transit.
  • Expansion of water treatment plants and flood control initiatives.
  • Digitized land administration and e-GIS mapping to boost transparency.
  • Upgraded 300+ public schools through the Lagos Education Transformation Project.

The next governor will be expected to build on these achievements while addressing urban planning challenges and fostering a more competitive business climate.

Beyond the APC primary, the 2027 Lagos governorship will also attract fierce opposition bids. The Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) and Labour Party (LP) — buoyed by the 2023 presidential wave — are already rebuilding structures. APC’s ultimate candidate must unite the party’s internal factions and appeal to a diverse, increasingly assertive urban electorate. With Lagos representing about 7 million registered voters, the race is set to be the most consequential off-cycle battleground.

2027 preview: The emergence of these five heavyweights elevates the Lagos governorship contest to a national-level spectacle. For the APC, it is a test of institutional strength and internal democracy. For Lagosians, it presents an opportunity to demand accountability and visionary leadership.

Sweep TV will continue tracking campaign developments, aspirant declarations, and the delicate negotiations that will shape Lagos’ next political chapter.

What comes next: party mechanisms and timeline

APC’s leadership at the state level is expected to convene stakeholder meetings by Q3 2026 to map out a transparent primary process. Whether the party opts for direct primaries or a consensus candidate remains fluid, but the caliber of aspirants suggests that any decision will require delicate balancing. Meanwhile, pressure groups aligned with each contender are already activating ward-level structures. The outcome will likely hinge on the interplay between state power brokers, the Presidency’s tacit leanings, and grassroots support across Lagos’ 20 Local Government Areas.

Key takeaway: Lagos 2027 is shaping up to be a high-stakes political drama with national implications — a race where ambition, legacy, and the future of Nigeria’s economic capital converge.
