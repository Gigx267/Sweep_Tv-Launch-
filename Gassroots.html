Inside APC: Grassroots Strength

The Foundation of Nigeria’s Ruling Party

The All Progressives Congress (APC) remains one of Nigeria’s most influential political forces, with a foundation deeply rooted in grassroots mobilization and national unity.

The Historic 2013 Mega-Merger 🇳🇬

Formed in 2013, the APC emerged from a historic merger of major opposition parties, including the Action Congress of Nigeria (ACN), Congress for Progressive Change (CPC), and All Nigeria Peoples Party (ANPP). This historic coalition was created with a single, clear objective: to challenge the long-standing dominance of the People’s Democratic Party (PDP) and offer Nigerians a fresh, progressive political direction.

ACN

Brought the administrative expertise and Southwest political machinery.

CPC

Contributed a massive, loyalist voter base across the Northern regions.

ANPP

Provided deep institutional experience and nationwide legislative structures.

Mobilizing the Common Citizen

From its inception, the APC leveraged strong grassroots structures inherited from its merging parties. With networks spanning across all 36 states, 774 local governments, and thousands of communities, the party quickly gained support among youths, traders, and political stakeholders nationwide.

Unlike traditional political entities that focus solely on urban centers, the APC’s strength lies in its Ward-Level Engagement. By empowering local leaders, the party ensures that its "Renewed Hope" message resonates at the very heart of the Nigerian electorate.

2015: A Historic Power Shift

The party’s defining moment came during the 2015 general elections when Muhammadu Buhari secured a historic presidential victory under the APC platform. This marked the first time an opposition party defeated an incumbent government in Nigeria, signaling a major shift in the country’s democratic journey and proving the effectiveness of grassroots mobilization.

2023 - 2026: The Renewed Hope Era

Since its initial victory, the APC has maintained a dominant presence in Nigeria’s political landscape, controlling the federal government and expanding its influence across multiple states. Under the current administration of President Bola Ahmed Tinubu, the party continues to promote its “Renewed Hope” agenda, focusing on aggressive economic growth, infrastructure development, and national stability.

The Pulse of Success

Political analysts attribute APC’s sustained success to its deep grassroots connections, strategic alliances, and ability to adapt to Nigeria’s evolving political climate. By prioritizing youth inclusion and digital registration, the party remains future-ready.

A Central Force in Democracy

As Nigeria looks ahead to future elections, the APC remains a central force shaping the nation’s governance and democratic process. Its ability to unify diverse regional interests under a single progressive banner continues to define the modern era of Nigerian politics.
