Governor Alia reaffirms voters’ role: “Benue people, not elites, will decide 2027”

Published March 22, 2026 · updated 3 hours ago | By Sweep TV political desk
Editorial focus: Governor Hyacinth Alia firmly rejects any attempt to direct Benue voters, insisting that performance and accountability will define his political fate — a strong counterpoint to Senator Titus Zam's remarks about SGF George Akume guiding the electorate.

Governor Hyacinth Alia of Benue State has drawn a clear line in the sand ahead of the 2027 governorship elections: the people of Benue alone hold the ultimate power to decide who leads them. In a sharp rebuttal to comments made by Senator Titus Zam, Alia's administration dismissed any notion that government officials or political godfathers could “guide” voters on their electoral choices. Through his Chief Press Secretary, Tersoo Kula, the governor declared that Benue citizens are not subjects of any external directive — they are the true sovereigns of the ballot.

Clash of political narratives

The tension erupted after Senator Titus Zam (representing Benue North-West) suggested in a recent outing that the Secretary to the Government of the Federation (SGF), Senator George Akume, would “guide the people of Benue on their voting decisions” in 2027. Zam’s remark, which many interpreted as an attempt to centralise electoral influence, triggered immediate pushback from the governor’s camp.

Kula’s statement: “No one can direct Benue voters”

In a strongly worded rejoinder, CPS Tersoo Kula stated: “Governor Alia has always maintained that the people of Benue State are the ultimate arbiters of their votes. No amount of political maneuvering or external directive can override the democratic rights of our citizens. The era of voting under duress or external instruction is long gone.”

Kula further stressed that Governor Alia’s political future will be determined solely by his performance, development projects, and service delivery across the state — not by endorsements or pressure from any political elite.

Political analysts weigh in: a defining moment

Political observers see Alia’s declaration as a strategic move that could bolster his credibility with ordinary voters. Dr. Chidi Odinkalu, a political analyst, noted: “Benue has a history of defying top-down electoral dictates. Alia is aligning himself with that tradition — betting that a performance-focused campaign will outlast any godfather’s influence.” Others point to the delicate relationship between the governor and SGF George Akume, both key APC figures in the state. Alia’s emphasis on voter autonomy may recalibrate internal party dynamics ahead of the primaries.

Civil society praise: “A victory for democratic accountability”

Benue Civil Society Network issued a statement applauding the governor’s stance: “We welcome Governor Alia’s unambiguous position that voters are not pawns. This reinforces the principle that electoral outcomes must reflect the people’s will, not the directives of appointees.” The group urged all political actors to respect the autonomy of Benue citizens.

Underlying tensions: Zam’s comments and the Akume factor

Senator Titus Zam’s original comment — which suggested that SGF George Akume would serve as a “guide” for Benue voters — triggered the current debate. While Zam later attempted to clarify that he meant political mentorship rather than coercion, Governor Alia’s camp seized the moment to reinforce a message of voter sovereignty. The exchange has exposed underlying currents within Benue APC: a faction that leans heavily on federal influence versus a reformist bloc led by Alia that champions popular mandate.

Political sources indicate that Alia’s approach may resonate with younger voters and rural communities who have grown weary of remote-controlled politics. By anchoring his future on tangible deliverables, the governor is attempting to future-proof his administration against backroom deals.

Tangible results: Alia’s record so far
  • Over 200km of rural roads rehabilitated across the three senatorial zones.
  • Revitalization of 12 primary healthcare centres with new equipment.
  • Free education policy extended to secondary schools, boosting enrollment.
  • Agricultural empowerment schemes reaching 5,000 smallholder farmers.

Governor Alia insists that this developmental record, not political endorsements, will speak to voters in 2027.

Benue State has long been a bellwether of independent voting patterns, with citizens often shifting allegiances based on performance, local government autonomy, and regional interests. Governor Alia’s current stance reinforces this tradition, positioning him as a leader who trusts the wisdom of the electorate. As the 2027 elections draw closer, all eyes will be on how the interplay between federal influence, party hierarchy, and grassroots sentiment unfolds.

“The will of Benue people is not negotiable.” — Governor Hyacinth Alia, through CPS Tersoo Kula.

This declaration marks a defining moment in Benue’s pre-election season, signaling that any attempt to command voter behaviour will be met with firm resistance from the government house.

Outlook: 2027 governorship race shifts toward accountability

With Alia’s emphasis on performance-based evaluation, the political discourse in Benue may pivot from personality cults to verifiable governance outcomes. Analysts predict that this could force other contenders to articulate clear policy platforms rather than rely solely on endorsements. As Senator Zam and SGF Akume recalibrate their strategies, Governor Alia appears determined to make the 2027 election a referendum on delivery — a move that could redefine political engagement in the state.
